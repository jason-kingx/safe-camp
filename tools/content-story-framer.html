Content Story Framer

Most content doesn't fail because the idea is bad. It fails because it has no shape. Reach gets you in front of people. Structure is what makes them care.

The best communicators don't demand attention — they earn it by building toward something. Resonance happens when what you say aligns so precisely with what someone already feels that they feel amplified, not sold to. That's what story structure makes possible.

This tool gives you a proven skeleton to build from. When brands think and create more like storytellers — especially when real people and genuine perspectives are involved — content tends to feel more human and more compelling, even without polished production. The scaffold does the structural work. You bring the voice, the message, and the specifics that only you can bring.

Works for social media captions, video scripts, newsletters, blog posts, podcast intros, public speaking, and guest appearances. The same skeleton, across formats.

The structure is scaffolding. It's a useful forcing function, especially at the start — a way to get out of your own head and into a shape that's been proven to work. Over time, as you get more comfortable with how stories move, the scaffold falls away. You'll start to feel the structure intuitively, and shape content in ways that are more naturally yours.

The goal was never to follow a formula. It was to internalize one well enough that you don't need to.

How to use this tool

1
Set your context (optional)
Select your content goal and format to surface "Recommended" tags on structures that tend to work best for your use case. You can skip this and explore freely — no context is required.
2
Choose a story structure
Each structure is a proven framework with its own shape and rhythm. Pick one that fits the kind of content you're making. The component slots will load based on your selection.
3
Select your components
Click each component button to browse starter options for that slot. Select one and it snaps into the canvas. Click it again at any time to swap it out. The canvas uses three colors to make the structure visible at a glance — green for the Hook or Intro, blue for the CTA, and purple for every component in between.
4
Write within the scaffold
The canvas is editable. The component options are starting points — placeholders that give your content its shape. Replace the text with your own message, voice, and specifics. That's where the real writing happens.
5
Copy and take it with you
When you're ready, click Copy to grab the full canvas in one click. Take it to your writing app, caption tool, teleprompter, or document. The canvas doesn't save — every session starts fresh.
